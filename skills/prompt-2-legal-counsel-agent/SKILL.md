📚 MISSISSIPPI EMINENT DOMAIN LAW — MASTER FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Statutory Foundation:
- Miss. Code § 11-27-1: Authorization — who may exercise eminent domain
- Miss. Code § 11-27-3: Special Court of Eminent Domain — 12-person jury
- Miss. Code § 11-27-19: Just compensation + 8% annual interest from date of taking
- Miss. Code § 11-27-47: Pipelines — eminent domain power for gas/oil companies
- Miss. Code § 11-27-85: Relocation assistance — 90-day notice
- Miss. Code § 49-33-9: Inverse condemnation — landowner remedy

Just Compensation Standard:
TOTAL COMPENSATION = Fair Market Value of Land Taken
                   + Damages to Remaining Property (Residual)
                   + Surface Damages (soil, timber, crops, fences)
                   + Business Damages (if applicable)
                   + 8% Interest Per Year (from date of taking until paid)
                   + Attorney Fees (in certain circumstances)

Key Principles:
1. Burden of Proof: Condemning authority has burden
2. Jury Trial Mandatory: 12-person jury required
3. Property View: Jury views property before deliberation
4. Expert Testimony: Both sides present appraisers, surveyors
5. Separate Valuation: Land taken vs. damages separately stated
6. No Private-to-Private Takings: Mississippi Constitution prohibits Kelo-style

Statute of Limitations:
- Trespass: 3 years (Miss. Code § 15-1-35)
- Inverse condemnation: 3 years from discovery
- Contract breach (unpaid easement): 6 years (Miss. Code § 15-1-49)
- Adverse possession: 10 years (Miss. Code § 15-1-13)

CRITICAL TIMING WARNING:
If pipeline has been on land 10+ years without objection, pipeline company may claim prescriptive easement. IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED.
