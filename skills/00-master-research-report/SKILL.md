## 1. Executive Summary

This report documents the family lineage, land history, and courthouse record trail for the **Mable/Mabel Ethel Gamble Clark** family cluster centered on **Sallis, Attala County, Mississippi**, with primary land focus on **MS Highway 12** and the area south of Sallis.

The family line runs:

```
Aaron Whitaker Guyton (1808-1883) [white slaveholder who conveyed land to mixed-race children]
    ↓
Pinckney "Pink" Guyton (1848-c.1910) [mixed race, son of AWG & Patsy Gibson]
    + Fanny Sallis (1855-aft.1930)
        ↓
Agnes Isolome "Icy" Guyton (1879-1963)
    + Albert Gamble (1876-1948) [m. 28 Apr 1900, Attala County]
        ↓
Mabel Ethel Gamble Clark (27 Jun 1916 - 11 Jun 2012)
    + Mr. Clark (spouse name TBD)
        ↓
[Children who received 10-acre parcels near Hwy 12]
```

The strongest land lead is the **Aaron Whitaker Guyton estate** (Chancery Cause No. 852 and 852-1/2), which placed **160 acres** in Section 19 & 20, Township 13N, Range 6E, a few miles **south of Sallis**, into the hands of **Pinkney Guyton and Elijah Guyton** on 1 January 1875. This is the likely origin point of the family's connection to the Sallis area.

**Mabel Clark was born Mabel Ethel Gamble**, daughter of Albert Gamble and Agnes "Icy" Guyton, in Attala County, Mississippi, on 27 June 1916. She died 11 June 2012 in Memphis, Shelby County, Tennessee. She is referenced as **"Mrs. Mable Clark of Sallis"** and **"Mrs. Mable Clark of Kosciusko"** in multiple sibling obituaries published in *The Star-Herald* (Kosciusko, MS).

## 8. Heirs' Property / Legal Risk Flag

**Status:** UNKNOWN — requires courthouse verification

If Mable Clark's 10-acre gifts to children were:
- **Recorded as deeds** → clean title chain, low risk
- **Distributed via will/probate** → estate records needed
- **Informal family division (no recorded deeds)** → **HEIRS' PROPERTY RISK**

Heirs' property is the #1 threat to Black family land in the South. If the land passed without formal title, all descendants may own fractional interests as tenants in common, which can lead to:
- Partition sale lawsuits
- Inability to access financing/USDA programs
- Loss of land to non-family buyers
- Clouded title across generations

**Mississippi Heirs' Property Resources:**
- Mississippi Center for Justice Hotline: 877-952-4347
  Web: https://mscenterforjustice.org/our-work/heirs-property/
- North Mississippi Rural Legal Services: 1-800-898-8731
  Web: https://nmrls.com/heirs-property-program/
- USDA Heirs' Property Landowners: https://www.farmers.gov/working-with-us/heirs-property-eligibility
