⚓ PART I — THE FOUNDATION: WHAT MARITIME LAW IS
Maritime law (a.k.a. admiralty law) governs activities on navigable waters — commerce, navigation, shipping, sailors, marine commerce, marine pollution, salvage, collision, towage, charterparties, marine insurance, piracy.

Constitutional anchor:

U.S. Const. Art. III, § 2, cl. 1 — extends federal judicial power "to all Cases of admiralty and maritime Jurisdiction"
28 U.S.C. § 1333 — grants federal district courts original jurisdiction over admiralty/maritime cases
"Saving to suitors" clause — preserves common-law remedies in state court for in personam claims
Two tests for admiralty jurisdiction:

Locality test — tort occurred on navigable waters
Nexus test — Executive Jet Aviation v. City of Cleveland, 409 U.S. 249 (1972); refined in Jerome B. Grubart v. Great Lakes Dredge, 513 U.S. 527 (1995): (a) potential disruption to maritime commerce + (b) substantial relationship to traditional maritime activity

⚓ PART V — THE PILLARS OF U.S. MARITIME DOCTRINE
1️⃣ Seaman's Remedies (the "trilogy")
(a) Jones Act negligence — 46 U.S.C. § 30104 — seaman sues employer for negligence; FELA-style "featherweight" causation standard (Rogers v. Missouri Pacific R.R., 352 U.S. 500 (1957))

(b) Unseaworthiness (general maritime law) — vessel owner's non-delegable duty; Mitchell v. Trawler Racer, 362 U.S. 539 (1960)

(c) Maintenance and cure (general maritime law) — daily living + medical until maximum cure; Aguilar v. Standard Oil, 318 U.S. 724 (1943); Vaughan v. Atkinson, 369 U.S. 527 (1962)

Seaman status test — Chandris v. Latsis, 515 U.S. 347 (1995); Harbor Tug v. Papai, 520 U.S. 548 (1997); ~30% time on vessel in navigation, employment-related connection substantial in nature/duration.

2️⃣ Non-Seaman Maritime Workers
LHWCA (33 U.S.C. § 901+) — longshoremen, harbor workers, shipbuilders
§ 905(b) — third-party negligence action against vessel; Scindia Steam Navigation v. De los Santos, 451 U.S. 156 (1981) (three duties: turnover, active operations, intervention)
Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act (OCSLA) — 43 U.S.C. § 1331 — extends LHWCA to OCS workers
3️⃣ Death
DOHSA (46 U.S.C. § 30301+) — wrongful death >3 nautical miles offshore; pecuniary damages only
Moragne v. States Marine Lines, 398 U.S. 375 (1970) — general maritime wrongful death cause of action territorial waters
Miles v. Apex Marine, 498 U.S. 19 (1990) — Miles uniformity principle (no non-pecuniary damages for Jones Act seamen)
Atlantic Sounding v. Townsend, 557 U.S. 404 (2009) — punitive damages allowed for willful M&C denial
4️⃣ Cargo (Carriage of Goods)
COGSA (46 U.S.C. § 30701 note) — applies tackle-to-tackle, $500/package limit, 1-year SOL
Harter Act (46 U.S.C. § 30701+) — pre-loading + post-discharge
Norfolk Southern v. Kirby, 543 U.S. 14 (2004) — Himalaya clauses, through bills of lading
5️⃣ Collision
COLREGS — international (33 U.S.C. § 1602)
Inland Rules — 33 U.S.C. § 2001+
Pennsylvania Rule — The Pennsylvania, 86 U.S. (19 Wall.) 125 (1873) — statutory violation shifts burden
Oregon Rule — The Oregon, 158 U.S. 186 (1895) — moving vessel hits stationary object = presumption of fault
Comparative fault — United States v. Reliable Transfer, 421 U.S. 397 (1975) (abolished divided damages rule)
6️⃣ Limitation of Liability
46 U.S.C. §§ 30501–30530: Owner may limit liability to post-casualty value of vessel + pending freight IF without privity or knowledge.

Norwich Co. v. Wright, 80 U.S. 104 (1871) — foundational
Lewis v. Lewis & Clark Marine, 531 U.S. 438 (2001) — interplay with saving-to-suitors
6-month filing deadline (§ 30529)
Small Passenger Vessel Liability Fairness Act of 2022 — eliminates limitation for vessels <100 GT carrying ≤49 overnight passengers domestically [Conception dive-boat fire response]
7️⃣ Salvage & General Average
Salvage Convention 1989 + 46 U.S.C. § 80101+
Voluntary, peril, success — three elements (The Sabine, 101 U.S. 384 (1879))
LOF (Lloyd's Open Form) — industry-standard "no cure, no pay" salvage contract
General Average — York-Antwerp Rules; voluntary sacrifice for common benefit
8️⃣ Maritime Liens & Ship Mortgages
Federal Maritime Lien Act (46 U.S.C. § 31301+) — "necessaries" provided to vessel create lien
Preferred Ship Mortgages (46 U.S.C. § 31322) — priority structure
Rule C (Supplemental Admiralty Rules) — in rem arrest of vessel
Rule B — quasi in rem attachment
9️⃣ Marine Insurance
Wilburn Boat v. Fireman's Fund, 348 U.S. 310 (1955) — state law fills gaps absent established federal admiralty rule
Great Lakes Insurance v. Raiders Retreat Realty, 601 U.S. 65 (2024) — choice-of-law clauses presumptively enforceable in marine insurance contracts
Hull, P&I (Protection & Indemnity), Cargo, Freight, Loss of Hire
🔟 Pollution
OPA-90 (33 U.S.C. § 2701+) — strict liability, removal costs + damages; Oil Spill Liability Trust Fund
CERCLA — overlapping for hazardous substances
CWA § 311 — discharge prohibition
Exxon Shipping v. Baker, 554 U.S. 471 (2008) — 1:1 punitive-to-compensatory ratio cap in maritime cases
