COMPLETE FCRA SECTION MAP
This is every single section of the Fair Credit Reporting Act, organized by FCRA section number AND U.S. Code citation — the two numbering systems used interchangeably in credit repair.

FCRA § U.S. Code (15 U.S.C.) Short Title What It Does
§ 601 — Short Title Names the statute "Fair Credit Reporting Act"
§ 602 § 1681 Congressional Findings & Purpose States that banking depends on fair/accurate credit reporting; CRAs must adopt reasonable procedures for accuracy, privacy, and fairness
§ 603 § 1681a Definitions & Rules of Construction Defines every key term: "consumer," "consumer report," "consumer reporting agency," "adverse action," "investigative consumer report," "medical information," "identity theft," "file," "firm offer of credit," "reseller," "nationwide specialty CRA," etc.
§ 604 § 1681b Permissible Purposes of Consumer Reports Lists the ONLY legal reasons someone can pull your credit: court order, consumer's written instructions, credit transaction, employment (with consent), insurance underwriting, government license/benefit, legitimate business need, account review, child support enforcement. Requires employer disclosure + written consent before pulling for employment.
§ 605 § 1681c Requirements on Information in Consumer Reports Time limits on negative info: most negatives fall off after 7 years; bankruptcies after 10 years; tax liens after 7 years from payment; collections/charge-offs 7 years from first delinquency. Prohibits reporting certain aged info.
§ 605A § 1681c-1 Identity Theft Prevention; Fraud Alerts & Active Duty Alerts Consumers can place initial fraud alerts (1 year), extended fraud alerts (7 years, with identity theft report), and active duty military alerts. CRAs must notify other nationwide CRAs. Users must take reasonable steps to verify identity before extending credit.
§ 605B § 1681c-2 Block of Information Resulting from Identity Theft Consumers who submit identity theft reports can request CRAs to block fraudulent tradelines. CRA must block within 4 business days. CRA can decline/rescind block if it determines the block was based on material misrepresentation or the consumer obtained goods/services.
§ 606 § 1681d Disclosure of Investigative Consumer Reports When someone orders an investigative report (with personal interviews), they must disclose this to the consumer within 3 days. Consumer has right to request disclosure of nature and scope of investigation.
§ 607 § 1681e Compliance Procedures CRAs must maintain reasonable procedures to: (a) limit furnishing reports to permissible purposes, (b) ensure maximum possible accuracy of information. Users must identify themselves and certify their permissible purpose.
§ 608 § 1681f Disclosures to Governmental Agencies CRAs may disclose identifying information (name, address, former addresses, employment) to government agencies.
§ 609 § 1681g Disclosures to Consumers Consumers have the right to know everything in their file: all information, sources of information, every entity that received a report in the past year (2 years for employment), credit scores and key factors, identity of anyone who requested a report. Free annual disclosures from nationwide CRAs.
§ 610 § 1681h Conditions & Form of Disclosure to Consumers CRAs must provide disclosures during normal business hours, by trained personnel, in person (with ID), by phone, or electronically. Consumer may be accompanied by one person of their choosing.
§ 611 § 1681i Procedure in Case of Disputed Accuracy THE CORE DISPUTE SECTION. When a consumer disputes info: CRA must conduct a reasonable investigation within 30 days (can extend to 45 if consumer provides additional info). CRA must forward all relevant info to the furnisher. Furnisher must investigate. If info can't be verified → must be deleted. CRA must notify consumer of results within 5 business days of completion. Consumer can add a statement of dispute to file. CRA must notify furnisher of deletion.
§ 612 § 1681j Charges for Certain Disclosures Free annual file disclosure from each nationwide CRA (via AnnualCreditReport.com). Free disclosure if adverse action taken, if consumer is unemployed and seeking employment, if on public assistance, if fraud alert placed. Otherwise max charge is $16.00 (effective Jan. 1, 2026).
§ 613 § 1681k Public Record Information for Employment Purposes CRAs that furnish public record info for employment must either notify the consumer or maintain strict procedures for accuracy.
§ 614 § 1681l Restrictions on Investigative Consumer Reports Investigative reports can't be prepared unless the consumer is clearly and accurately told in writing within 3 days that the report may be made, and the consumer has the right to request additional disclosures.
§ 615 § 1681m Requirements on Users of Consumer Reports (Adverse Action) ADVERSE ACTION RULES. Any person who takes adverse action based on a consumer report MUST: (1) provide notice of the adverse action, (2) name the CRA that provided the report, (3) state that the CRA did not make the decision, (4) inform consumer of right to get free copy and dispute. Applies to credit denials, insurance, employment, etc. Also covers risk-based pricing notices and prescreened offers.
§ 616 § 1681n Civil Liability for Willful Noncompliance Damages for WILLFUL violations: actual damages OR statutory damages of $100–$1,000 per violation, PLUS punitive damages as court allows, PLUS attorney's fees and costs.
§ 617 § 1681o Civil Liability for Negligent Noncompliance Damages for NEGLIGENT violations: actual damages PLUS attorney's fees and costs. (No statutory damages or punitive damages for negligence.)
§ 618 § 1681p Jurisdiction of Courts; Limitation of Actions Lawsuits can be filed in any federal district court without regard to amount in controversy, or in any state court of competent jurisdiction. Statute of limitations: 2 years from discovery, or 5 years from occurrence — whichever is earlier.
§ 619 § 1681q Obtaining Information Under False Pretenses Criminal penalty: Anyone who knowingly obtains a consumer report under false pretenses — fined under Title 18 and/or imprisoned up to 2 years.
§ 620 § 1681r Unauthorized Disclosures by Officers or Employees Criminal penalty: Any CRA officer or employee who knowingly provides info to unauthorized persons — fined under Title 18 and/or imprisoned up to 2 years.
§ 621 § 1681s Administrative Enforcement CFPB has primary enforcement authority. FTC enforces against non-banks. Federal banking regulators enforce against their supervised institutions. State AGs can bring actions in federal court.
§ 622 § 1681s-1 Information on Overdue Child Support Obligations State/local child support agencies can report overdue child support to CRAs.
§ 623 § 1681s-2 Responsibilities of Furnishers of Information to CRAs FURNISHER DUTIES — HUGE FOR DISPUTES. (a) Duty to provide accurate info; duty to correct and update; duty not to report info the furnisher knows is inaccurate; duty to provide notice of disputes to CRAs; duty to provide negative info notice to consumers. (b) Duties upon notice of dispute from CRA: must conduct investigation, review all relevant info, report results to CRA, if inaccurate → modify/delete/permanently block. (c) Limitation: No private right of action for § 623(a) — only § 623(b) (after CRA forwards dispute).
§ 624 § 1681s-3 Affiliate Sharing (Opt-Out) Consumers can opt out of affiliated companies using their credit information for marketing solicitations. Affiliates must provide clear opt-out notice.
§ 625 § 1681t Relation to State Laws FCRA generally does not preempt state laws EXCEPT in specific areas listed (like the format of §§ 605A and 605B procedures, prescreening, fraud alerts, etc.). States CAN provide greater consumer protections in many areas.
§ 626 § 1681u Disclosures to FBI for Counterintelligence Purposes FBI can obtain certain consumer info for counterintelligence investigations with written certification.
§ 627 § 1681v Disclosures to Governmental Agencies for Counterterrorism Government agencies can obtain consumer reports for international terrorism investigations with written certification.
§ 628 § 1681w Disposal of Records Anyone who maintains or possesses consumer information must properly dispose of it to protect against unauthorized access.
§ 629 § 1681x Corporate and Technological Circumvention Prohibited Can't use corporate structure or technology tricks to evade the requirements of the FCRA.

DISPUTE LETTER TEMPLATES — KEY FCRA CITATIONS
Round 1 — Initial Bureau Dispute
Key laws cited: § 611 (§ 1681i), § 607(b) (§ 1681e(b)) Purpose: Request investigation of inaccurate items; CRA has 30 days to investigate and verify or delete

Round 2 — Method of Verification Request
Key laws cited: § 611(a)(6)-(7) (§ 1681i(a)(6)-(7)) Purpose: After CRA says "verified" — demand they tell you the method of verification, the name/address/phone of furnisher who verified, and what info was provided

Round 3 — Warning / Intent to Sue Letter
Key laws cited: § 616 (§ 1681n), § 617 (§ 1681o), § 618 (§ 1681p) Purpose: Put CRA/furnisher on notice of potential lawsuit for willful or negligent noncompliance

Furnisher Direct Dispute
Key laws cited: § 623(a)(8) (§ 1681s-2(a)(8)), § 623(b) (§ 1681s-2(b)) Purpose: Dispute directly with the creditor/collector reporting the inaccurate info

Debt Validation (FDCPA + FCRA combo)
Key laws cited: FDCPA § 809 (15 U.S.C. § 1692g) + FCRA § 623 Purpose: Demand validation of debt from collector AND dispute the reporting simultaneously

Cease & Desist + Dispute Combo
Key laws cited: FDCPA § 805(c) (15 U.S.C. § 1692c(c)) + FCRA § 611 Purpose: Stop collector communication AND dispute the tradeline
